# sales-profitability-dashboard
**Excel + PostgreSQL + Power BI | Kaggle Superstore Dataset**

Analyzing $7M in revenue across 4 regions, 3 categories, 
and 3 channels to find where the business makes money and where discounts are quietly destroying profit.

# The Business Problem
A retail company tracks $7M in revenue across Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies. 
The numbers look healthy. But underneath, three serious problems are hiding.

The company has all three problems — and without a clear view of profitability by customer, category, region, and channel, leadership cannot tell which parts of the business are actually worth investing in.

This dashboard makes profitability visible across customers, categories, regions, and channels.

## Key Business Insights

**Insight 1 - Furniture category suffers from weak profitability**

Furniture generates strong revenue contribution but delivers the lowest profit margin across all categories. Heavy discounting, significantly compresses profitability despite healthy sales volume.

**Insight 2 - High discounting destroys profit margins**

Orders discounted above 30% generate extremely low or even negative profit margins, indicating that aggressive discounting is driving revenue growth at the expense of profitability.

**Insight 3 - South region underperforms due to excessive discounts**

The South region records the lowest profit margin despite stable sales performance. Higher average discount rates compared to other regions are significantly reducing profitability.

**Insight 4 - Office Supplies drives volume but weak order value**

Office Supplies generates the highest order volume but maintains the lowest Average Order Value (AOV), indicating strong demand for lower-ticket products and frequent purchasing behavior.

**Insight 5 — Revenue growth does not always translate into profitability**

Several high-revenue categories and regions underperform in profit margin due to aggressive discounting strategies. This indicates that revenue alone is not a reliable indicator of business health.

## Business Recommendations

**1. Implement discount caps for Furniture products* * - Introduce category-level discount thresholds for Furniture products, especially within the Tables sub-category. Transactions exceeding predefined discount limits should require approval-based pricing controls. The company should also review product pricing and supplier costs for consistently low-margin SKUs.

2. Set a hard cap on high-discount transactions** -  Implement a maximum allowable discount cap of 30% across all categories and customer segments. Any exception beyond this threshold should require managerial approval supported by volume justification or strategic customer value.

3. 3. Optimize pricing and discount strategy in the South region - Gradually reduce average discount exposure in the South region and evaluate whether certain customer segments are receiving unnecessary pricing concessions. Regional pricing policies should be reviewed alongside:
-customer profitability,
-repeat purchase behavior,
-and product mix performance.

4. Increase Average Order Value in Office Supplies - Introduce product bundling, volume incentives, and cross-selling strategies to encourage customers to increase basket size.
The company can also test:
-minimum order thresholds,
-combo offers,
and premium Office Supplies bundles.

5. Improve profitability in high-volume, low-margin segments -The business should prioritize customers, products, and regions that generate stronger profit contribution rather than focusing only on sales volume growth.
Improving profit per order and increasing transaction value would strengthen overall business profitability without relying solely on acquiring additional customers or increasing sales volume.
